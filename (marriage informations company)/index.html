Project Background:
This project was inspired by the declining birthrate in South Korea. Currently, matchmaking agencies already exist in Korea, helping people find partners based on social status, economic background, and other criteria. If the birthrate continues to fall, these agencies could evolve in more extreme and dystopian ways: ranking people, commodifying love, and selling marriage as a product. This concept sparked the idea for the project, exploring a speculative scenario where reproduction is tightly controlled and love is increasingly transactional.

About the Work:
Birth rates are decreasing year by year in South Korea, leading to fewer and fewer babies. In this project, the Ministry of Reproduction is formed, where the government controls matchmaking, pairing people into ideal couples based on social status and encouraging them to procreate. The work imagines a society where reproduction is regulated, love is transactional, and human relationships are reduced to bureaucratic processes—a dystopian reflection of the pressures and anxieties surrounding fertility and marriage.
